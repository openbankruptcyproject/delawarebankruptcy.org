Medical Debt and Bankruptcy in Delaware

How bankruptcy eliminates medical debt in Delaware. Chapter 7 vs Chapter 13, medical bills in collections, hospital liens, and your rights.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Medical Debt Is Fully Dischargeable

Medical debt is classified as general unsecured debt under bankruptcy law. It has no special protections and is fully eliminated in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy. In Delaware, as in every state, the federal Bankruptcy Code controls how medical debt is treated.

  • Chapter 7: Medical debt is wiped out in 3-4 months with no repayment
  • Chapter 13: Medical debt is included in your repayment plan; you may pay only a fraction of what you owe, and any remaining balance is discharged
  • No minimum amount: You can file regardless of how much medical debt you have

Medical Debt in Delaware: Key Facts

Before Filing

  • Medical bills can be sent to collections
  • Providers can sue and obtain judgments
  • Judgment liens may attach to your property
  • Wages can be garnished (15% of disposable earnings (more protective than federal 25%))
  • Credit score damage from unpaid bills

After Filing

  • Automatic stay stops all collection activity
  • Lawsuits are halted immediately
  • Garnishment stops
  • Judgment liens may be avoidable (section 522(f))
  • Discharge eliminates the debt permanently

Chapter 7 vs Chapter 13 for Medical Debt

Chapter 7 - Quick Elimination

If you qualify for Chapter 7 (pass the means test), medical debt is eliminated in 3-4 months with no repayment required. This is usually the fastest and simplest option for medical-debt-only situations.

Chapter 13 - Structured Repayment

If your income is above the means test threshold, or if you need to protect assets that exceed Delaware's exemptions, Chapter 13 lets you repay a portion of your medical debt over 3-5 years. Any remaining balance is discharged at plan completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy eliminate medical debt in Delaware?

Yes. Medical debt is unsecured debt and is fully dischargeable in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy. It is one of the most common reasons people file for bankruptcy nationwide.

Is medical debt treated differently in bankruptcy?

No. Medical debt is treated the same as credit card debt, personal loans, and other unsecured obligations. It receives no special priority and is eliminated in the discharge. There is no minimum or maximum amount of medical debt required to file.

Can I file bankruptcy just for medical bills?

Yes. You do not need to have multiple types of debt to file. Many people file bankruptcy solely because of medical bills. Medical debt is the leading cause of bankruptcy filings in the United States.

What happens to medical debt in collections?

Medical debt that has been sent to a collection agency is still dischargeable. Once you file, the automatic stay stops all collection activity. After discharge, the debt is eliminated and the collector cannot contact you again.

Can a hospital put a lien on my house for medical bills?

In Delaware, hospitals and medical providers can sometimes obtain a judgment lien on your property if they sue you and win. However, such liens may be avoidable in bankruptcy if they impair your homestead exemption under 11 U.S.C. section 522(f).

Will bankruptcy stop medical debt lawsuits?

Yes. Filing bankruptcy immediately triggers the automatic stay, which halts all pending lawsuits including medical debt collection cases. If a judgment has already been entered, the underlying debt is still dischargeable.
