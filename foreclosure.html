Stop Foreclosure with Bankruptcy in Delaware

How bankruptcy stops foreclosure in Delaware. Delaware uses judicial foreclosure (approximately 6 months or longer). Chapter 13 mortgage catch-up, Chapter 7 timeline, and your options.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Foreclosure in Delaware

Type: Judicial foreclosure

Typical timeline: approximately 6 months or longer

Judicial foreclosure requires the lender to file a lawsuit, which gives you more time to prepare. However, once a judgment is entered, the sale can proceed quickly.

How Bankruptcy Stops Foreclosure

Chapter 7

  • Immediate stop: Automatic stay halts foreclosure
  • Duration: Stay lasts 3-4 months (until case closes)
  • After case: Lender can resume foreclosure
  • Best for: Buying time or surrendering the home
  • Deficiency: Mortgage deficiency is discharged

Chapter 13

  • Immediate stop: Automatic stay halts foreclosure
  • Duration: Stay lasts 3-5 years (entire plan)
  • Arrears cure: Catch up on missed payments through plan
  • Best for: Saving your home long-term
  • Lien stripping: May eliminate junior liens

Chapter 13 Mortgage Cure

How It Works

If you are $12,000 behind on your mortgage, Chapter 13 lets you spread that $12,000 over your 3-5 year plan. On a 60-month plan, that is $200 per month in arrears payments on top of your regular mortgage payment. During the plan, the lender cannot foreclose as long as you make both payments.

Requirements

  • You must have regular income sufficient to fund the plan
  • Current mortgage payments must be made on time during the plan
  • Arrears payments must be completed within the plan period
  • You must complete all plan payments to receive a discharge

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy stop foreclosure in Delaware?

Yes. Filing either Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 immediately triggers the automatic stay, which halts all foreclosure proceedings. Delaware uses judicial foreclosure, which typically takes approximately 6 months or longer.

How does foreclosure work in Delaware?

Delaware uses judicial foreclosure. This means the lender must file a lawsuit in court to foreclose, which generally takes longer and gives you more time. The typical timeline is approximately 6 months or longer.

Is Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 better for stopping foreclosure?

Chapter 13 is generally better for saving your home because it allows you to catch up on missed payments over 3-5 years. Chapter 7 only delays foreclosure temporarily - once the case is closed (3-4 months), the lender can resume foreclosure proceedings.

How does Chapter 13 cure mortgage arrears?

Chapter 13 lets you catch up on missed mortgage payments (arrears) through your 3-5 year repayment plan while making current mortgage payments going forward. The total arrears are spread over the plan period, making catch-up affordable.

What if I cannot afford my mortgage?

If your mortgage is unaffordable, you may be able to negotiate a loan modification during your bankruptcy case. If the home is not worth saving, you can surrender it in Chapter 7 and discharge the mortgage deficiency, or in Chapter 13 you can surrender and have the deficiency treated as unsecured debt.

What is the homestead exemption in Delaware?

Delaware's homestead exemption protects up to $125,000 in home equity. If your equity is within the exemption, your home is protected from the bankruptcy trustee in Chapter 7.
