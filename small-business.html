Small Business Bankruptcy in Delaware

Guide to small business bankruptcy in Delaware. Chapter 7 liquidation, Chapter 11 Subchapter V, Chapter 13 for sole proprietors, and protecting your business.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Small Business Bankruptcy Options in Delaware

Chapter 7 - Liquidation

  • Business closes and assets are sold
  • Fastest resolution (3-4 months)
  • Sole proprietors receive personal discharge
  • LLCs/corps do NOT receive a discharge
  • Filing fee: $338

Subchapter V (Ch. 11)

  • Streamlined reorganization
  • Debts under $7,500,000
  • Owner retains equity
  • No creditor committee
  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Filing fee: $1,738

Traditional Chapter 11

  • Full reorganization for larger businesses
  • More expensive and complex
  • Creditor committee involvement
  • Greater court oversight
  • Filing fee: $1,738

Chapter 13 (Sole Proprietors)

  • Available only to sole proprietors
  • Debt limits apply (currently ~$2.75M)
  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep business assets
  • Filing fee: $313

Subchapter V - The Game Changer

The Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 created Subchapter V of Chapter 11, specifically designed for small businesses. It has fundamentally changed small business bankruptcy by making reorganization accessible and affordable.

Key Advantages

  • Speed: Plan must be filed within 90 days (vs. 18+ months in traditional Chapter 11)
  • Cost: No creditor committee, fewer contested matters, lower attorney fees
  • Owner retains equity: The absolute priority rule does not apply - owners can keep their interest without paying unsecured creditors in full
  • Debtor-in-possession: You continue running your business during the case
  • Consensual or cramdown: Plan can be confirmed even without creditor consent if it commits all disposable income for 3-5 years

Filing in the District of Delaware

Courthouse: 824 Market St, Wilmington, DE 19801

Small business cases in the District of Delaware are assigned a Subchapter V trustee who facilitates the reorganization process. The trustee's role is to help the debtor and creditors reach a consensual plan, not to liquidate assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are my options for small business bankruptcy in Delaware?

Small businesses in Delaware can file Chapter 7 (liquidation), Chapter 11 (reorganization), Chapter 11 Subchapter V (streamlined for small businesses with debts under $7,500,000), or Chapter 13 (for sole proprietors with debts under the limit). The best choice depends on whether you want to continue or close the business.

What is Subchapter V bankruptcy?

Subchapter V of Chapter 11 (created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019) is a streamlined reorganization for businesses with debts under $7,500,000. It is faster, cheaper, and more debtor-friendly than traditional Chapter 11. There is no committee of unsecured creditors, and the owner can retain equity without paying creditors in full.

Can I keep my business if I file bankruptcy in Delaware?

Yes, if you file Chapter 11 or Subchapter V. These chapters allow you to reorganize debt while continuing to operate. Chapter 13 also works for sole proprietors. In Chapter 7, the business typically closes unless it has no non-exempt assets.

What happens to business debts if I am a sole proprietor?

Sole proprietors are personally liable for all business debts. Filing personal bankruptcy discharges both personal and business debts. If your business is a corporation or LLC, only the entity's debts are addressed in the entity's bankruptcy - your personal liability depends on whether you personally guaranteed the debts.

Can I file bankruptcy for my LLC?

Yes, LLCs can file Chapter 7 (liquidation) or Chapter 11/Subchapter V (reorganization) in the District of Delaware. However, LLCs do not receive a discharge in Chapter 7 - the entity is simply liquidated. Subchapter V is typically the best option for an LLC that wants to continue operating.

How much does small business bankruptcy cost?

Chapter 7 business filing fee is $338. Chapter 11 Subchapter V filing fee is $1,738. Attorney fees in Delaware for Subchapter V typically range from $10,000 to $30,000 depending on complexity. Chapter 7 business attorney fees typically range from $2,000 to $5,000.
