Tax Debt and Bankruptcy in Delaware

Can you discharge tax debt in Delaware bankruptcy? IRS rules, the 3-year/2-year/240-day tests, priority vs. non-priority taxes, and state tax obligations.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Which Tax Debts Can Be Discharged?

Not all tax debts are dischargeable in bankruptcy. To qualify for discharge, income tax debts must pass ALL of the following tests:

  1. 3-Year Rule: The tax return was due at least 3 years before filing (including extensions)
  2. 2-Year Rule: The tax return was actually filed at least 2 years before filing bankruptcy
  3. 240-Day Rule: The tax was assessed at least 240 days before filing
  4. No fraud or willful evasion: The return was not fraudulent and you did not willfully attempt to evade the tax
  5. Return was filed: You actually filed the tax return (unfiled returns cannot be discharged)

Priority vs. Non-Priority Tax Debt

Priority Tax Debt (NOT Dischargeable)

  • Recent income taxes (within 3 years)
  • Trust fund taxes (payroll taxes withheld from employees)
  • Taxes assessed within 240 days
  • Must be paid in full in Chapter 13
  • Survives Chapter 7 discharge

Non-Priority Tax Debt (Dischargeable)

  • Income taxes meeting the 3/2/240-day tests
  • Taxes on returns filed without fraud
  • Eliminated in Chapter 7 discharge
  • Treated as general unsecured in Chapter 13
  • May pay only a fraction through the plan

Delaware Tax Considerations

Delaware has a graduated state income tax with rates from 0% to 6.6%.

Delaware state income taxes generally follow the same discharge rules as federal taxes. To discharge Delaware state tax debt, the same 3-year, 2-year, and 240-day tests must be met.

Tax liens: Both federal and state tax liens survive bankruptcy. The lien remains on property you owned at the time of filing, even though your personal liability is discharged. This means the IRS or state can still collect from that specific property, but cannot pursue you personally for the debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy discharge tax debt?

Yes, certain tax debts can be discharged in bankruptcy if they meet specific age and filing requirements. Income taxes that are more than 3 years old, were filed on time (or more than 2 years ago if filed late), and were assessed more than 240 days ago may be dischargeable.

What is the 3-year rule for tax debt in bankruptcy?

The tax return must have been due at least 3 years before the bankruptcy filing date (including extensions). For example, a 2022 tax return due April 15, 2023 would meet the 3-year rule after April 15, 2026.

What is the 2-year rule for tax debt?

If you filed the tax return late, it must have been filed at least 2 years before the bankruptcy filing. If you never filed the return, the tax debt cannot be discharged.

What is the 240-day rule?

The IRS must have assessed the tax at least 240 days before the bankruptcy filing. Assessment typically occurs when you file your return or when the IRS completes an audit. Certain events (like an offer in compromise) can toll this period.

What about Delaware state taxes?

Delaware state income taxes follow similar rules to federal taxes for bankruptcy discharge. Delaware has a graduated state income tax with rates from 0% to 6.6%. State tax liens, like federal tax liens, survive bankruptcy even if the underlying tax debt is discharged.

What happens to tax liens in bankruptcy?

Tax liens survive bankruptcy even if the underlying tax debt is discharged. The lien remains attached to property you owned at the time of filing. However, the personal obligation to pay is eliminated, so the IRS can only collect from the specific property to which the lien attached.
