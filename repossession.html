Stop Repossession in Delaware with Bankruptcy

How bankruptcy stops vehicle repossession in Delaware. Get your car back, Chapter 13 cramdown, reaffirmation, and your rights under Delaware law.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Stopping Repossession in Delaware

The moment you file bankruptcy in the District of Delaware, the automatic stay takes effect and all repossession activity must stop. If a repo agent is literally on the way to take your car, filing stops them in their tracks.

Delaware vehicle exemption: $15,000 in equity per debtor

Before vs. After Repossession

Car Not Yet Repossessed

  • Automatic stay prevents repossession
  • File before the repo happens
  • Continue making payments (reaffirm) or use Chapter 13 to catch up
  • Vehicle stays in your possession

Car Already Repossessed

  • File immediately -- before the car is sold
  • Court may order turnover under section 542
  • Lender must stop the sale process
  • Chapter 13 can structure catch-up payments
  • If already sold, deficiency is dischargeable

Your Options by Chapter

Chapter 7 Options

  • Reaffirmation: Keep paying the loan as before. Car is yours but debt survives.
  • Redemption: Pay the car's current fair market value in a lump sum.
  • Surrender: Give back the car and discharge the remaining balance.

Chapter 13 Options

  • Catch up on payments: Spread missed payments over 3-5 year plan.
  • Cramdown: Reduce loan balance to current value (if purchased 910+ days ago).
  • Lower interest rate: Court may approve a lower rate than your original loan.
  • Surrender: Give back the car and treat deficiency as unsecured debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy stop repossession in Delaware?

Yes. Filing bankruptcy immediately triggers the automatic stay, which stops all repossession attempts. If you file before the vehicle is repossessed, the lender cannot take it. Delaware's vehicle exemption protects up to $15,000 in equity.

Can I get my car back after repossession in Delaware?

If your car has been repossessed but not yet sold, filing bankruptcy may force the lender to return it. The automatic stay requires the lender to stop all collection activity, and courts in the District of Delaware may order turnover of the vehicle under 11 U.S.C. section 542.

How does Chapter 13 help with repossession?

Chapter 13 lets you keep your vehicle and catch up on missed payments through your 3-5 year plan. If the vehicle was purchased more than 910 days ago, you can cramdown the loan to the car's current value, potentially saving thousands.

What is the 910-day rule for car loans?

If you purchased your car more than 910 days (about 2.5 years) before filing Chapter 13, you can reduce the loan balance to the vehicle's fair market value. If purchased within 910 days, you must pay the full loan balance.

What happens if my car is repossessed and sold?

If the car has already been sold, you may owe a deficiency balance (the difference between what the car sold for and what you owed). This deficiency is unsecured debt and can be discharged in bankruptcy.

What is Delaware's vehicle exemption?

Delaware's vehicle exemption protects up to $15,000 in equity. If your equity is within this amount, the bankruptcy trustee cannot sell your vehicle in Chapter 7.
