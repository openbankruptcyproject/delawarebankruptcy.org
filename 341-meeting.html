The 341 Meeting of Creditors in Delaware

What to expect at the 341 meeting of creditors in Delaware. Location, questions asked, what to bring, and how to prepare for your hearing in the District of Delaware.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting of creditors is a required part of every bankruptcy case, named after 11 U.S.C. section 341. It is your one mandatory appearance in the bankruptcy process.

Location: 824 Market St, Wilmington, DE 19801

When: Approximately 30-40 days after your bankruptcy petition is filed

Duration: Typically 5-10 minutes for routine consumer cases

What to Bring

  • Valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)
  • Social Security card or other proof of SSN (W-2, SSA-1099, or IRS letter)
  • Copy of your bankruptcy petition and schedules
  • Most recent federal tax return
  • Recent pay stubs (last 60 days)
  • Bank statements (last 6 months) -- bring copies in case the trustee requests them

Common Questions the Trustee Will Ask

  1. Did you read and sign your bankruptcy petition and schedules?
  2. Is everything in your petition true and correct?
  3. Have you listed all of your assets?
  4. Have you listed all of your debts?
  5. Have you sold, transferred, or given away any property in the last 2 years?
  6. Are you expecting any tax refunds?
  7. Do you have any pending lawsuits, insurance claims, or inheritance?
  8. Is there anything you would like to change or correct in your petition?

Key tip: Answer honestly and briefly. If you do not know the answer, say so. Your attorney will be present and can help guide you.

What Happens After

Chapter 7

  • Trustee has 60 days to file objections
  • If no objections, discharge enters ~60 days after meeting
  • Case closes shortly after discharge
  • Total: ~3-4 months from filing to discharge

Chapter 13

  • Plan confirmation hearing follows
  • Trustee reviews plan feasibility
  • Creditors may file objections to plan
  • Court confirms plan (typically months 2-4)
  • Plan payments continue for 3-5 years

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 341 meeting in Delaware?

The 341 meeting (named after 11 U.S.C. section 341) is a required hearing where the bankruptcy trustee and any creditors can ask you questions under oath about your finances, assets, and petition. In the District of Delaware, these meetings are held at 824 Market St, Wilmington, DE 19801.

What questions will the trustee ask?

Common questions include: Did you review your petition and schedules? Is everything accurate? Have you listed all your assets and debts? Have you transferred any property in the last 2 years? Do you have any pending lawsuits or inheritance claims? Are you expecting a tax refund?

What should I bring to the 341 meeting?

Bring: a valid government-issued photo ID, your Social Security card (or other proof of SSN), your most recent tax return, recent pay stubs, and copies of your bankruptcy petition and schedules. Your attorney will advise on any additional documents.

How long does the 341 meeting last?

Most 341 meetings last 5-10 minutes. The trustee asks a standard set of questions, and unless there are complications or creditor objections, the meeting is brief. Complex cases or cases with significant assets may take longer.

Do creditors actually attend the 341 meeting?

In most consumer cases, creditors do not attend. Occasionally, a creditor may appear to ask about specific property or debts, but this is uncommon. You should be prepared for questions from any creditor, but do not be surprised if none show up.

What happens after the 341 meeting?

After the 341 meeting, the trustee has 60 days to object to your discharge or file motions regarding your assets. If no objections are filed, your Chapter 7 discharge is typically entered about 60 days after the meeting. In Chapter 13, plan confirmation proceedings continue.
