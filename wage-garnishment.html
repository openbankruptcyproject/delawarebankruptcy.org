Stop Wage Garnishment in Delaware

How bankruptcy stops wage garnishment in Delaware. Delaware garnishment limits, the automatic stay, and recovering garnished wages.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Delaware Garnishment Laws

Garnishment limit: 15% of disposable earnings - more protective than the federal standard of 25%

Garnishment can take a significant portion of your paycheck, making it difficult to cover basic expenses like rent, utilities, and food. Filing bankruptcy provides immediate relief through the automatic stay.

How Bankruptcy Stops Garnishment

Garnishment Stopped By Bankruptcy

  • Credit card debt garnishment
  • Medical bill garnishment
  • Personal loan garnishment
  • Deficiency balance garnishment (repossession, foreclosure)
  • Old utility bill garnishment
  • Judgment debt garnishment

Garnishment NOT Stopped

  • Child support - domestic support obligations continue
  • Alimony - spousal support continues
  • Student loans - unless undue hardship is shown
  • Certain tax debts - priority tax obligations
  • Criminal restitution - court-ordered restitution

Recovering Garnished Wages

If wages were garnished within 90 days before your bankruptcy filing and the total exceeds $600, the bankruptcy trustee may recover those wages as a preferential transfer under 11 U.S.C. section 547. These recovered funds go into the bankruptcy estate but may benefit you in certain circumstances.

In Chapter 13, your repayment plan accounts for garnished wages and may provide additional recovery options.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much of my wages can be garnished in Delaware?

In Delaware, creditors can garnish 15% of disposable earnings - more protective than the federal standard of 25%. Filing bankruptcy immediately stops all garnishment through the automatic stay.

Does bankruptcy stop wage garnishment immediately?

Yes. The moment you file bankruptcy, the automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 takes effect. Your employer must stop withholding garnished wages once notified. Most courts provide electronic notice within 24-48 hours of filing.

Can I get back wages that were garnished?

In some cases, yes. If wages were garnished within 90 days before filing and exceed $600, the trustee may be able to recover them as a preferential transfer under 11 U.S.C. section 547. In Chapter 13, you may be able to recover garnished wages through your plan.

What types of garnishment can bankruptcy stop?

Bankruptcy stops garnishment for credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and most other unsecured debts. It does NOT stop garnishment for child support, alimony, most student loans (unless undue hardship is shown), and certain tax debts.

How long does it take to stop garnishment after filing in Delaware?

The automatic stay takes effect instantly upon filing. Most employers in the District of Delaware stop garnishment within 1-3 business days of receiving notice from the court. If garnishment continues after notice, that is a stay violation.

What if my employer continues garnishing after I file?

Continued garnishment after bankruptcy filing is a violation of the automatic stay. Document the violation and notify your attorney. You may be entitled to damages under section 362(k). Courts in the District of Delaware take stay violations seriously.
