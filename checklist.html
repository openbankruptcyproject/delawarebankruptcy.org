Bankruptcy Filing Checklist for Delaware

Complete checklist for filing bankruptcy in Delaware. Documents needed, steps to take, and what to expect before, during, and after filing in the District of Delaware.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Before Filing

  • Complete credit counseling course from a U.S. Trustee-approved agency (within 180 days of filing)
  • Gather tax returns for the last 2 years
  • Collect pay stubs for the last 6 months
  • Obtain bank statements for the last 6 months (all accounts)
  • List all debts with creditor names, account numbers, and current balances
  • List all assets with current values (home, vehicles, personal property, investments)
  • Obtain a current credit report from annualcreditreport.com
  • Gather vehicle titles and registration documents
  • Collect mortgage and home equity statements
  • Gather proof of all income sources
  • Document monthly expenses (rent/mortgage, utilities, food, transportation, insurance)
  • List any property transfers made in the last 2 years
  • Note any lawsuits, garnishments, or collection actions pending against you

Things to Avoid Before Filing

  • Do not transfer property -- Transferring assets to friends or family within 2 years of filing can be reversed by the trustee as a fraudulent transfer
  • Do not pay back family preferentially -- Payments to family members within 1 year can be recovered by the trustee as preferential transfers
  • Do not take on new debt -- Incurring debt you know you cannot repay may be considered fraud
  • Do not drain retirement accounts -- Retirement funds are generally protected in bankruptcy; do not spend them on debts
  • Do not hide assets -- Failure to disclose assets is a federal crime and will result in denial of discharge
  • Do not make large credit card purchases -- Charges over $800 for luxury goods within 90 days of filing are presumed non-dischargeable

Filing Day

  • Petition and schedules filed with the District of Delaware
  • Filing fee paid ($338 Chapter 7 / $313 Chapter 13) or installment plan requested
  • Credit counseling certificate filed with petition
  • Automatic stay takes effect immediately
  • Case number assigned
  • Trustee assigned
  • 341 meeting date scheduled (approximately 30-40 days out)

After Filing

  • Notify your attorney of any creditor contact (automatic stay violations)
  • Prepare for 341 meeting of creditors
  • Bring photo ID and Social Security proof to 341 meeting
  • Complete debtor education course (required before discharge)
  • File certificate of debtor education with the court
  • Continue making any required payments (mortgage, car, Chapter 13 plan)
  • Respond to any trustee requests for additional documents
  • Await discharge order (Chapter 7: ~90-120 days; Chapter 13: after plan completion)

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to file bankruptcy in Delaware?

You need: tax returns (last 2 years), pay stubs (last 6 months), bank statements (last 6 months), a list of all debts with account numbers and balances, vehicle titles and registration, mortgage statement, proof of insurance, and a credit report. You also need a certificate of completion from an approved credit counseling agency.

How much does it cost to file bankruptcy?

Chapter 7 filing fee is $338 and Chapter 13 filing fee is $313. Attorney fees vary. Both chapters require two credit counseling courses ($25-$50 total). Filing fee waivers or installment plans may be available for those who qualify.

What should I NOT do before filing bankruptcy?

Do not: transfer property to friends or family, pay back family members preferentially, take on new debt, hide assets, drain retirement accounts to pay debts, or make large purchases on credit. These actions can jeopardize your case or result in denial of discharge.

Do I need a lawyer to file bankruptcy?

While you can file pro se (without a lawyer), bankruptcy is complex and mistakes can be costly. An attorney ensures your exemptions are properly claimed, your petition is accurate, and your rights are protected throughout the process in the District of Delaware.

What is credit counseling?

Federal law requires two courses: (1) credit counseling before filing (must be completed within 180 days before filing), and (2) debtor education after filing (must be completed before discharge). Both must be from agencies approved by the U.S. Trustee's office.

How do I find my local bankruptcy court in Delaware?

The bankruptcy court for the District of Delaware is located at 824 Market St, Wilmington, DE 19801. Filing is typically done electronically by your attorney. Pro se filers can file in person at the clerk's office.
