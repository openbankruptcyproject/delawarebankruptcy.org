Bankruptcy Timeline in Delaware

Step-by-step timeline for filing bankruptcy in Delaware. From credit counseling to discharge, what happens and when in the District of Delaware.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Chapter 7 Timeline

Weeks 1-4: Preparation

Gather financial documents: tax returns (2 years), pay stubs (6 months), bank statements (6 months), vehicle titles, mortgage statements, list of all debts. Complete required credit counseling course.

Day 1: Filing

Petition filed with the District of Delaware. Automatic stay takes effect immediately. All creditor collection stops. Case number and trustee assigned.

Days 30-40: 341 Meeting

Meeting of creditors at 824 Market St, Wilmington, DE 19801. Trustee asks questions about your finances under oath. Typically lasts 5-10 minutes. Bring photo ID and Social Security card.

Days 40-60: Debtor Education

Complete the second required course (debtor education / financial management). Must be completed before discharge can be entered.

Days 90-120: Discharge

Court enters discharge order approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. All dischargeable debts are eliminated. The discharge injunction permanently bars creditors from collecting.

Days 120-150: Case Closed

Trustee files final report. Case is officially closed. Chapter 7 remains on credit report for 10 years from filing date.

Chapter 13 Timeline

Weeks 1-4: Preparation

Gather financial documents and complete credit counseling. Attorney drafts repayment plan based on your income, expenses, and debts.

Day 1: Filing

Petition and proposed plan filed. Automatic stay takes effect. Must begin plan payments within 30 days of filing.

Days 30-40: 341 Meeting

Meeting of creditors. Trustee reviews your plan and finances. Creditors may file objections.

Months 2-4: Plan Confirmation

Court hearing to confirm your repayment plan. Objections are resolved. Judge approves (or modifies) the plan.

Years 1-5: Plan Payments

Make monthly plan payments to the Chapter 13 trustee for 3-5 years. Trustee distributes payments to creditors. Stay current on mortgage and car payments.

Plan Completion: Discharge

After completing all plan payments and the debtor education course, the court enters your discharge. Any remaining unsecured debt balances are eliminated.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Chapter 7 take in Delaware?

Chapter 7 in the District of Delaware typically takes 3-4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting happens about 30-40 days after filing. The discharge is entered approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting.

How long does Chapter 13 take in Delaware?

Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan. Plan confirmation usually happens 2-4 months after filing. After completing all plan payments, the discharge is entered. The entire process takes 3-5 years.

What happens at the 341 meeting of creditors?

The 341 meeting (also called the meeting of creditors) is a brief hearing where the bankruptcy trustee and any creditors can ask you questions under oath about your finances, assets, and bankruptcy petition. In the District of Delaware, these meetings typically last 5-10 minutes.

How long before I can rebuild my credit?

You can start rebuilding credit immediately after discharge. Most people qualify for secured credit cards right away. FHA mortgages are available 2 years after Chapter 7 discharge. Conventional mortgages require a 4-year wait.

When does the automatic stay take effect?

The automatic stay takes effect the instant your bankruptcy petition is filed with the court. All creditor collection activity must stop immediately. Electronic filing means most cases are filed and effective within minutes.

How long does it take to prepare a bankruptcy filing?

Gathering documents and preparing the petition typically takes 1-4 weeks. You need tax returns, pay stubs, bank statements, a list of all debts and assets, and completion of the required credit counseling course.
